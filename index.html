Previous Project: CEMS (Continuous Emission Monitoring System)

The Continuous Emission Monitoring System (CEMS) is an innovative environmental initiative launched by J-PAL South Asia at IFMR in association with the Gujarat Pollution Control Board and the South Gujarat Textile Park Association. This project aims to provide real-time data on industrial emissions, ensuring regulatory compliance and promoting sustainable practices.

  • For more information. Click Here

    Project Overview:

    • Objective: To accurately measure and report industrial emissions through the implementation of CEMS, enhancing pollution control and regulatory compliance.
    • Scope: Deploying CEMS across multiple industrial sites in South Gujarat, enabling precise monitoring and reporting of emissions.
    • Stakeholders: Industrial units, Gujarat Pollution Control Board, South Gujarat Textile Park Association, environmental agencies, and technology providers.

    Jay's Role and Responsibilities:

    • Leading a team of 10 professionals in project execution and coordination.
    • Overseeing the installation, calibration, and connectivity of CEMS at participating industrial sites.
    • Analyzing emissions data to identify trends, issues, and opportunities for improvement.
    • Collaborating with stakeholders to ensure compliance with CEMS regulations and guidelines.
    • Providing strategic recommendations based on data analysis to enhance the effectiveness of the CEMS.
    • Supporting the development of cost-effective ESG compliance goals for participating industries.

    Project Impact:

    The CEMS project is expected to significantly improve the accuracy of emissions reporting and regulatory compliance in South Gujarat. By leveraging expertise in data analysis, project management, and ESG compliance, Jay Bhagat is contributing to the successful implementation of this critical environmental initiative.
